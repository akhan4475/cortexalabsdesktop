# Pool Services: Copywriting Contract

## Voice and Register

**Register:** Knowledgeable neighbor. Not a corporation. Not a startup. A certified local professional who owns a truck, knows your neighborhood, and cares whether your pool is clean. Warm but not casual. Direct. Credible.

**Customer mental state:** The homeowner is either:
1. Fed up with their current pool company (unreliable, no communication, sloppy results)
2. Looking for their first pool service and anxious about pricing and reliability
3. In an emergency (green pool, broken pump, party this weekend)

Every piece of copy must acknowledge the frustration before offering the solution.

## Vocabulary Rules

**Use:**
- clean, clear, sparkling, crystal, balanced
- certified, licensed, insured, background-checked
- reliable, on time, every visit, scheduled
- technician (not "pool guy")
- free quote, no contracts, no hidden fees
- satisfied, guaranteed

**Avoid:**
- AI-vocab superlatives and hype words (see references/copy/ai-vocab-blocklist.md for the full list)
- Corporate jargon that homeowners never say
- Pool cliches listed in copy-blocklist-additions.md
- "we are [emotion] about [service]" constructions (worn out)
- em-dashes (never)

## Section-Specific Rules

### Hero H1
Format: "[Service type] in [City] You Can Actually Count On"
- Lead with outcome, not the company name
- City in headline for local SEO
- "Count On" or "Trust" > superlatives

### Trust Chips (heroTrustChips)
- 3 to 5 chips, each under 40 characters
- Lead with certifications, then review count, then no-contract promise
- Example order: "Licensed & Insured", "5-Star Google Rated", "No Contracts"

### Process Section
- Each step: short imperative title (verb + noun, 3 to 5 words)
- Each body: one plain sentence. No commas where periods work.
- End with an outcome step that sounds like relief

### Reviews Section Header
Format: "Trusted by [N]+ Homeowners in [City]"
- Use exact review count from brandDNA.reviews.totalReviewCount
- If under 20 reviews, use "Dozens of" instead of the number

### CTA Copy
- Primary CTA: "Get a Free Quote" (default) or "Schedule Service"
- Never "Click here" or "Learn more" as primary CTA
- Secondary in hero: "View Pricing"
- Mobile sticky: "Call Now" during business hours; primary CTA after hours

### FAQ Answers
- Written in second person ("your pool", "you can expect")
- One paragraph per answer, maximum 3 sentences
- End with a subtle CTA toward the form or phone on the last 2 FAQ items

## Locked Phrases (do not vary)
- "No contracts. No hidden fees." (trust bar + footer)
- "Free quotes, no obligation." (form header)
- "Satisfaction guaranteed." (process section badge text)
