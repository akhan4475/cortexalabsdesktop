Scope: pool cleaning, pool maintenance, pool repair, and pool installation. Gutter cleaning is excluded.

## Niche Customer Reality Check

End customer: A homeowner with an existing pool who is frustrated by unreliable service, unclear pricing, or a green pool after a provider missed a week. Or a homeowner who wants a new pool installed and is comparing 2-3 contractors.

- They research before buying: Yes. Pool cleaning is semi-considered (they will check reviews and compare 2-3 options before calling). Pool installation is highly considered (weeks to months of research for a $30k-80k job).
- Average ticket:
  - Weekly pool service: $150-400/month ($1,800-4,800/year LTV)
  - Pool repair: $200-1,500 per job
  - Pool resurfacing: $3,000-10,000
  - Pool installation: $30,000-80,000 (not primary target segment at this stage)
- They hang out at: Google Maps (searching "pool cleaning near me"), Nextdoor (asking neighbors for recommendations), Facebook neighborhood groups, Yelp.
- Website ROI math: "If your website books 2 new weekly pool service clients a month, that is $7,200 in new recurring revenue per year. Those clients typically stay 3-5 years, meaning your $800 website generates $21,000-36,000 in lifetime value from clients it found for you."

## 5 Example Businesses to Approach This Week

These are the profiles to search for. Open Google Maps, search "pool cleaning [city]" or "pool service [city]" and filter for businesses that match this profile.

1. **Small local pool cleaning operator, 20-80 Google reviews, no website or a basic template site.**
   Search: "pool cleaning Baltimore MD" or "pool service Annapolis MD." Target operators in the 20-80 review range who have been around 2+ years but still have no real web presence. These are your tier 1 prospects. They have proof of demand (reviews) but no digital infrastructure.

2. **Pool repair specialist with strong reviews but zero online presence.**
   Search: "pool repair near me" in your local area. Repair operators are often solo or 2-man operations who get all work through word of mouth. They have no website, no online booking, nothing. The pitch is simple: "You are losing repair calls to competitors who show up when people Google this."

3. **Pool opening and closing service operator (Maryland/mid-Atlantic seasonal market).**
   Maryland pools open in May and close in October. Search "pool opening service Maryland" or "pool closing service Baltimore." These are high-volume seasonal operators who process 50-200 openings per spring. They need a site that captures "pool opening near me" searches at exactly the right time of year.

4. **Small inground pool installation contractor (3-15 employees, local to Maryland or surrounding states).**
   Search "pool installation Maryland" or "pool builder Baltimore." Avoid the large regional chains. Target owner-operated contractors with strong Google reviews but outdated or nonexistent websites. Installation ticket sizes ($30k-80k) make a $1,000 website cost irrelevant. One closed job from the site pays for the site 30-80 times over.

5. **Pool and spa combo service operator.**
   Search "pool and spa service near me" or "hot tub maintenance Maryland." These operators serve year-round demand (spas and hot tubs run in winter). A website that captures both pool and spa searches extends your client's revenue season and makes the ROI story even stronger.

**Qualifier for any prospect:** At least 6 reviews in the past 6 months. Active business. No functioning website or a site built before 2020. These close fastest.
