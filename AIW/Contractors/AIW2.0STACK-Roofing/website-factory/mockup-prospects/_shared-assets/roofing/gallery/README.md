# Roofing Gallery — Stock Photo Drop Zone

Drop photos here before recording a Loom mockup. The `quick-mockup.py` script
reads this folder and loads images into the live template.

## Folder structure

| Folder | What goes in it |
|--------|-----------------|
| `before-after/` | Before/after pairs — name them `01-before.jpg`, `01-after.jpg` etc. |
| `team/` | Headshot or candid of the owner or crew. Well-lit, 400px+ square. |
| `action/` | Crew on a roof, ladders up, trucks with a visible logo, material stacks. |
| `hero/` | Wide horizontal shots — completed roofs, dramatic sky + shingles, aerial. |

## Free stock photo sources for roofing

- **Unsplash** — search "roofing", "roof replacement", "shingles", "contractor"
- **Pexels** — search "roofer", "roof tiles", "construction worker roof"
- **Freepik** — search "roofing team", "asphalt shingles", "roofing contractor"
- **StockSnap** — search "metal roof", "residential roofing"

## Naming convention

Keep filenames lowercase with hyphens. The template renders them in alphabetical order.

```
before-after/01-before.jpg
before-after/01-after.jpg
before-after/02-before.jpg
before-after/02-after.jpg
team/owner.jpg
action/crew-01.jpg
action/truck-01.jpg
hero/hero-01.jpg
hero/hero-02.jpg
```

## Minimum for a convincing mockup

- 2 before/after pairs (shows transformation = trust)
- 1 team photo (owner preferred — makes the mockup feel real)
- 2 action shots (shows active company)
- 1 hero image (wide, high quality — dominates the fold)

## What makes a good roofing stock photo

- Workers actually on a roof (not staged ground-level poses)
- Visible shingles or metal panels up close
- Finished residential roofs — dark grey, charcoal, or black shingles convert best
- No generic construction hard hats without roofing context
- Avoid photos with logos or watermarks of other companies
