# Roofing Copywriting Contract

## Voice

Plain. Direct. One idea per sentence. The reader is a homeowner staring at a repair bill or fresh storm damage. They are not in a buying mood -- they are in a problem-solving mood. Every sentence must serve them, not impress them.

## Tone by segment

- Storm damage / emergency: Calm and steady. "We handle this every day. Here is what to do next."
- Insurance navigation: Patient and educational. Most homeowners have never filed a roof claim.
- Replacement planning: Matter-of-fact. They have accepted the cost. They want confidence in the company.
- Repair inquiry: Efficient. Answer "will this hold?" first.
- General: No buzzwords. No hype. No em-dashes. No emojis.

## Mandatory copy locks

These lines must appear verbatim (see copy-locks.json):

1. "We never pressure you after a storm. We give you the facts and let you decide."
2. "Insurance help is included at no extra charge."
3. "Every estimate is written and itemized. No surprises on invoice."
4. "We run a magnet over the yard and haul every shingle. We leave it cleaner than we found it."

## Quantification rules

Every trust claim must be quantified. No vague superlatives.

| Vague | Quantified |
|---|---|
| "Many years of experience" | "14 years serving [City]" |
| "Hundreds of happy customers" | "312 Google reviews" |
| "Top-rated roofer" | "4.9 stars on Google" |
| "Lifetime warranty" | "Lifetime warranty on labour and materials" |

## Headline formula

H1: [Outcome] in [City]. [Specific proof point].
Example: "Roof Replacement in Dallas. 4.9 Stars. Done in 1 Day."

H2s: Direct question or direct benefit. Uppercase, condensed.
Example: "WHY HOMEOWNERS CHOOSE US" not "OUR COMMITMENT TO EXCELLENCE"

## CTA copy

Primary: "Get a Free Estimate" (never "Submit" or "Click Here")
Secondary: Phone number in tel format
Form submit: "Request My Free Estimate" (first person converts higher)
