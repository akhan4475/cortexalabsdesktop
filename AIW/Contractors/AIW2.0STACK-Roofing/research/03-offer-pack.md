## Section 1: Positioning

### Full Positioning Sentence

"I help roofing companies win the trust of homeowners who are getting multiple quotes after a storm or a leak so they choose my client over the three other roofers that called them back."

### One-Liner (public Q&A benchmark)

"I build websites for roofing companies that turn review-checkers into estimate requests."

Alternative: "I build trust-first websites for roofers -- the kind that makes a homeowner pick up the phone."

**Which to use:** The first. It is specific. "Turn review-checkers into estimate requests" names the exact behavior homeowners exhibit before choosing a roofer.

### Why This Positioning Works

The homeowner's decision moment is the review-check. They have already decided to hire a roofer. The website's only job is to make sure it is your client they call. Every element of the site -- star count in the hero, license number in the nav, before/after photos, AI chatbot for insurance questions -- closes that gap between "I found them on Google" and "I pressed call."

## Section 8: The 15-Minute Call Script (What Happens After They Reply)

When a prospect replies to a DM:

1. "Great -- let me send you a Calendly link for a 15-minute call. We'll look at your current site together and I'll show you the roofing template I've built."
2. On the call: share screen. Show the roofing template (run `npm run dev` locally). Show them what their site could look like.
3. At 12 minutes: "Here is how it works. I do the hero design first, free. You tell me if you like it. If you do, we talk about the rest."
4. Do not close on the call. Close in the follow-up 24 hours later: "Here is the hero I built for you. Want to move forward?"
