NICHE SYNTHESIS, pool-gutter

Score from Module 2A: 22/35

End customer in one sentence: A homeowner who needs recurring maintenance for their pool or gutters and is frustrated by "hit or miss" services -- they want reliability, transparent pricing, and proof that the job was done.

The student's client in one sentence: A small pool cleaning or gutter cleaning company with 1-10 employees, doing $100k-600k/year in recurring residential work, with no website or a template website that gets zero leads.

---

Top 5 Insights:

1. Reliability is the entire brand promise. The phrase "hit or miss" appears explicitly in reviews as a description of the industry. Every positive review praises showing up, doing what was said, and communicating. This is a low bar the industry consistently fails to clear. A website that leads with reliability signals (documentation, photos, guarantee) wins immediately.

2. Before/after photo documentation is a conversion feature, not a nice-to-have. "Before and after pictures = peace of mind" is a verbatim customer quote. Building photo documentation into the service offer -- and showing it on the website -- directly addresses the #1 customer anxiety (was the job actually done?).

3. Online booking is table stakes in this niche. "The way they have their online booking and payment set up is so nice and easy" was called out specifically in a review. Competitors without online booking lose to those who offer it. A website with a booking widget (or link to Jobber/ServiceAutopilot) is a real differentiator for small operators still taking calls only.

4. Pool cleaning in Sun Belt markets is the most defensible segment. Year-round demand, $2,400-4,800/year per client LTV, and national chains (Pinch A Penny, America's Swimming Pool Company) compete on price, not local trust. A local operator with a strong website + GMB can own the Maps Pack in a specific city.

5. The niche is genuinely split. Pool clients and gutter clients are different buyers with different seasonal patterns and service expectations. A website that addresses both clearly (separate service pages, separate seasonal CTAs) outperforms a generic "home services" site.

---

Top 3 Risks if We Pick This Niche:

- Lower ticket size than roofing. The ROI pitch for a gutter cleaning client is weaker ($300-600/year LTV vs. $9,000/job for a roofer). Pool cleaning is stronger ($3,600+/year LTV) but requires targeting Sun Belt markets specifically.
- Seasonal demand (gutter). Gutter cleaning peaks in fall and spring. Winter months slow to near zero in cold climates. Not an issue for pool in Phoenix but real for gutter-only clients in northern markets.
- National chain competition in pool. Pinch A Penny and America's Swimming Pool Company have franchise locations and national SEO presence. Local operators compete on Maps Pack positioning, not organic rankings.

---

Top 3 Reasons to Pick This Niche:

- Ayaan owns a pool. Genuine insider knowledge of the service, the expectations, and the pain of finding a reliable provider.
- Recurring revenue model. Every client is a long-term relationship worth $2,400-4,800/year (pool) or $300-600/year (gutter). The lifetime value story is strong.
- Market fragmentation. Most pool and gutter companies are tiny, unsophisticated, and have weak websites. The competitive bar is very low. A clean, trust-first website with online booking is a top-tier product in this field.

---

Money math headline: "If your website books 2 new weekly pool service clients a month, that's $7,200 in new recurring revenue per year -- and those clients typically stay 3-5 years, meaning your $800 website generates $21,000+ in lifetime value."
