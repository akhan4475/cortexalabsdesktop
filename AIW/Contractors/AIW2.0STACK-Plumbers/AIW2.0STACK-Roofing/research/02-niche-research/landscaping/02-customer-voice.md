## Decision Moments

1. **Design consultation moment.** Multiple reviews mention the landscaper recommending design changes the customer hadn't considered. The website must position the business as a design expert, not just a labor provider.
2. **Irrigation or maintenance emergency.** Same-day response wins the initial job; ongoing relationship converts to retainer. Website must have a prominent "emergency service" or "same-day availability" signal.
3. **Visual before/after decision.** Landscaping is a visual purchase. Customers decide based on seeing past work. Portfolio/gallery is a primary conversion tool.
4. **Referral + review confirmation.** Customers say they found the company through a neighbor or Google, then confirmed with reviews. Both the Map listing and the website gallery matter.

---

## Fears (Top 5)

1. **Sloppy or incomplete work.** The 1-star review ("half-baked job, refused to fix") captures the dominant fear. Homeowners fear paying for work that looks bad or fails.
2. **Poor communication about scope.** "Miscommunications about what exactly we want done" -- customers fear not being heard or misunderstood.
3. **Not showing up or going over timeline.** "In on time" is praised specifically because lateness is expected. The fear is a crew that disappears mid-project.
4. **Hidden costs.** "Reasonably priced" is praised, implying the baseline fear is being surprised by a bill higher than the quote.
5. **Not knowing what you're getting.** "Sal recommended design changes better than anything I could have thought of" -- customers don't know what they want. They fear making a bad design decision.
