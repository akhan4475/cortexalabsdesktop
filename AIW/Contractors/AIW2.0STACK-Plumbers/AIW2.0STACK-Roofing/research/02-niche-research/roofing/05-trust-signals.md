## Top 5 Trust Signals (Priority Order)

### 1. Google Review Count + Recent Star Rating
**Justification:** Multiple end-customer reviews explicitly mention "read reviews before calling." The DallasLocalSEO Reddit post confirms a roofer with a 2009-era website still ranks #1 because of review volume. Review count and recency are the single highest-frequency trust signal observed across all sources. A roofer with 50+ reviews and 4.8+ stars wins the Google Maps decision before the homeowner even visits the website.

**What to build:** Google reviews widget (embedded or screenshot tiles), total review count in hero, review recency visible (past 6 months).

### 2. GAF / Owens Corning Manufacturer Certification
**Justification:** Every winning agency site mentions GAF or Owens Corning certification. These are industry-specific credibility signals that homeowners have been trained to look for. GAF Master Elite means the roofer is in the top 3% of GAF contractors. Owens Corning Platinum is equivalent. These badges appear in hero sections, not buried in footers.

**Specific badges to display:**
- GAF Master Elite Contractor
- GAF Weather Stopper Roofing Contractor
- Owens Corning Platinum Preferred Contractor
- CertainTeed SELECT ShingleMaster

**What to build:** Badge bar in or immediately below hero.

### 3. BBB Accreditation (A+ Rating)
**Justification:** BBB appears in SERP results ("BBB can help you find BBB Accredited roofing contractors"). Homeowners actively use BBB to vet contractors before hiring. A+ rating with zero complaints is a meaningful differentiator.

**What to build:** BBB badge with clickable link to verified profile.

### 4. Licensed + Insured Statement with License Number
**Justification:** Fear of unlicensed work (and being left liable for accidents) is a real homeowner concern. Showing the actual license number -- not just claiming "licensed" -- converts better [inferred from review patterns emphasizing trust in specifics]. This is especially critical in states with public contractor license lookup tools.

**What to build:** "Licensed & Insured" badge with state license number. Link to license verification page if available.

### 5. Before/After Photo Gallery with Named Projects
**Justification:** Reviews praise visual quality ("roof looks beautiful," "roof looks great"). Homeowners making a $8-12k decision want to see the work. Unnamed stock photos do not convert. Named local projects ("Dallas homeowner, Mockingbird Heights, 2024") are significantly more credible. [inferred from review specificity patterns]

**What to build:** Gallery with 6-12 before/after pairs. Each pair labeled with city/neighborhood and service type. No stock images.

## Press / "As Seen In" Expectations
Not expected in this niche. Local roofers do not have national press. [inferred] If the client has been featured in local news (storm coverage, community stories), that is worth including. Otherwise, skip.

---

## Photo/Video Testimonial Expectations
- Video testimonials are a differentiator but not expected. If available, use. If not, written reviews with photo of reviewer (Google-sourced) are sufficient.
- Before/after project photos are more conversion-relevant than testimonial videos in this niche.
