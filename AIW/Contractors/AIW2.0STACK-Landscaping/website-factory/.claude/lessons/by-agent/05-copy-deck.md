## Niche Voice Seed: Landscaping

These rules are seeded from Module 2D niche research and customer voice analysis.
They are not corrections -- they are the niche-specific starting point for every landscaping copy run.

### Voice register
- Register: calm and competent. Never excited. Never breathless.
- Perspective: "we" throughout. Not "I", not "the team", not "our crew" (too casual).
- Sentence length: short. One idea per sentence. Trust reads fast.

### Preferred terms (use these, not synonyms)
- Project noun: "yard" (not "outdoor space", "lawn area", "landscape")
- Client noun: "homeowner" (not "client", "customer", "resident")
- Outcome verb: "transform" (not "improve", "enhance", "elevate", "upgrade")
- Quality adjective: "thoughtful" (used sparingly -- not in every paragraph)
- Credential noun: "licensed and insured" (not "fully licensed", "properly insured")

### Banned words (from niche playbook + universal blocklist)
leverage, synergize, seamless, game-changer, cutting-edge, robust, stunning, breathtaking, luxury,
passion, dedication, excited, thrilled, innovative, state-of-the-art, world-class, best-in-class,
elevate, enhance, empower, holistic, curated, bespoke, artisanal

### Copy rules derived from customer voice research
- Mention responsiveness directly. It is the #1 stated differentiator in reviews (9x frequency).
  Use "We respond within 2 hours" or "Same-day response" -- not "we're always available".
- Name the license number explicitly in the FAQ and in the license badge context. Vague "licensed and insured" is weaker than "TX contractor license #XXXX".
- FAQ answers must include a price range for the cost question. Do not write "it depends on the scope" without a number.
- Process steps must not exceed 3. Homeowners do not trust a 5-step process from a landscaper.
- Owner story must NOT use the word "passion". It must explain what the owner SAW that others got wrong and what they do differently.
- Why-choose bullets must start with a verb ("We Show Up On Time", not "Reliability" or "On-Time Service").
- Avoid referencing "the disruption" of construction unless the copy explicitly reassures around it. If mentioned, immediately follow with the reassurance ("and we leave the site cleaner than we found it").

### Segment vocabulary (use in service page copy, not homepage)
- Residential design segment pain: "You have a yard you ignore because it is hard to know where to start."
- Residential design desire: "A yard that looks cared for, feels inviting, and adds real value to your home."
- Lawn maintenance pain: "Keeping up with lawn care takes time you don't have."
- Hardscaping pain: "My outdoor space isn't usable -- no patio, no structure."
- Hardscaping desire: "A patio, walkway, or outdoor kitchen that extends my living space."
