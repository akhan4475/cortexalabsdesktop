# SOP — Stage 6: Copy Deck (Landscaping)

Implements: Stage 6 (Copywriting).
Niche: landscaping

---

## Purpose

Generate the full site copy for a landscaping client. Every headline, body paragraph, FAQ answer, service card, and CTA string in the delivered website comes from this stage. Copy must be written in the landscaping voice defined in `niche-playbook/copywriting.md` and obey the universal copywriting skill contract.

## Procedure

### Step 0 — Read accumulated lessons

Read `.claude/lessons/by-agent/05-copy-deck.md` if it exists. Apply all rules as overrides to this SOP.

### Step 1 — Read the universal copywriting skill

Read `.claude/skills/copywriting/SKILL.md`. All universal copy rules apply. Niche-specific rules in `niche-playbook/copywriting.md` take precedence on any conflict.

### Step 2 — Read niche voice

Read `templates/landscaping/niche-playbook/vocabulary.json`. Internalize:
- Voice register: calm and competent
- Banned words list (never use in any copy)
- Segment vocabulary per service type

### Step 3 — Apply copy locks

From `niche-playbook/copy-locks.json`, these strings are LOCKED and must appear verbatim in brand-dna output:
- formHeader: "Get Your Free Landscape Estimate"
- submitButton: "Request My Free Estimate"
- privacyLine: "We never share your info. No spam. No hard sell."
- mobileCallLabel: "Call Now"

Do not paraphrase or "improve" locked copy. Write it exactly.

### Step 4 — Generate hero copy

H1 pattern: "Transform Your [City] Yard"
Subheadline: address reliability. Reference "on time", "responsive", or "done right". Under 18 words.
Write 2 H1 variants and 2 subheadline variants. Mark the recommended option.

### Step 5 — Generate service copy

For each service in `research.json` services array:
- Name: plain, no adjective stacking
- Body: one sentence. Describe the outcome, not the process. Under 25 words.

### Step 6 — Generate why-choose-us items

Write 4 items. Each: direct benefit claim, under 8 words.
Pattern: "[We do X]" — the outcome is implied.
Avoid generic phrases like "committed to excellence" or "passionate about quality".

### Step 7 — Generate founder copy

Para 1: how and why they started. Personal, first-person for the quote but third-person in the narrative.
Para 2: what they are proud of. No buzzwords.
Vision sentence: what they are trying to build. Under 20 words.
Mission sentence: how they operate day to day. Under 20 words.

### Step 8 — Generate FAQ answers

6 questions. Required topics: cost estimate, free estimate, timeline, insurance, service area. One additional niche-relevant question from research.
Answers: 2-4 sentences. Direct. No hedging phrases like "it depends" without a follow-up answer.

### Step 9 — Write to brand-dna fields

Output a JSON object matching the brand-dna copy shape. Save to `Pipeline Data/copy/copy-deck.json`.

## Pass criteria

- No banned words from `vocabulary.json` in any generated copy
- All locked strings from `copy-locks.json` are verbatim
- Hero H1 contains the client city name
- FAQ has exactly 6 entries
- No sentence exceeds 30 words

## When this stage halts

- Research data missing: go back to Stage 2
- Client city not identified in research: ask the student before proceeding

---

## Landscaping niche notes

The homeowner buying landscaping is a visual buyer who anxious about reliability. Every sentence should remove one anxiety. Do not try to excite them. Calm them. Show competence and specificity.

City name in the H1 is non-negotiable. It is both SEO and trust — local landscapers beat national services because they know the neighborhood.

Portfolio and before/after copy should describe what was BUILT, not how beautiful it is. "Design and install: 2,400 sq ft backyard with flagstone patio, garden beds, and sod" beats "Stunning transformation of this gorgeous Dallas backyard."
