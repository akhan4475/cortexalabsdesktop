# Module 3 - Offer Pack: Landscaping

**Student:** Ayaan Khan
**Niche:** Landscaping (design/install, 1-20 employees, $200k-2M/year)
**Date:** 2026-07-01

---

## 1. Positioning Sentence

**One sentence. This is what you say when someone asks what you do.**

> "I build websites for landscaping companies that show up on Google, display their work properly, and turn site visitors into booked estimate calls -- including an AI chatbot that captures leads after hours."

**When to use it:**

- Cold DM opener (paraphrased, not copy-pasted)
- Instagram bio
- When a prospect asks "what do you do exactly?"

**What makes it work:**

- "Show up on Google" -- speaks to the Maps Pack problem every landscaper has
- "Display their work properly" -- hits the portfolio gap (every landscaper has photos, almost none have a gallery that converts)
- "Booked estimate calls" -- the outcome they care about, not "website traffic"
- "AI chatbot that captures leads after hours" -- the differentiator no landscaping web agency offers

---

## 2. Stage Check

**Before pitching, confirm these 3 things about any prospect:**

1. Does the business have fewer than 100 Google reviews OR reviews that have gone cold (none in the last 30 days)? If yes, the Maps signal is weak and a new site alone will not fully solve it. Note this -- the site is still worth buying, but you should mention GMB optimization as a bonus.
2. Does their current site have a portfolio/gallery section with real project photos? If yes, can you still do better (faster, cleaner, mobile-first)? If their portfolio is already excellent, this is a harder sell.
3. Are they a design/install operator or a pure lawn-mowing service? Maintenance-only clients have lower ticket sizes and may balk at a $1,500 website. Focus on install operators.

**If all three criteria lean your way, pitch.**

---

## 3. Offer Structure

**What you sell:**

A done-for-you landscaping website. One price. No retainer required.

**What's included:**

- Custom Vite + React website built on the landscaping template (dark forest green, Fraunces headings, mobile-first)
- Full project portfolio gallery (before/after layout) populated with their existing photos
- 6 service pages (Landscape Design, Lawn Maintenance, Hardscaping, Irrigation, Sod, Tree Care -- swap for their actual services)
- Service area pages for up to 5 cities (local SEO)
- Google Maps + Google Reviews integration (star rating, review count, embedded reviews)
- AI chatbot (Voiceflow) wired to capture lead name, phone, zip, and service interest -- routes to their email or CRM
- Contact form with Make.com webhook to their inbox
- Deployed to Vercel. They get the domain, you handle the deploy.
- FAQ section targeting "how much does landscaping cost [city]" queries

**Price:**

$1,200 - $1,500 one-time. (Anchor at $1,500, be willing to $1,200 if they're your first 2-3 clients and you need the case study.)

**Optional upsell (after delivery):**

$150/month -- hosting, chatbot maintenance, monthly photo/review update. Position as "keeping the site growing."

**Delivery time:** 5-7 days from receiving their assets (photos, logo, business info).

## 4. Cold DM Templates

These are for Instagram DM, Facebook Messenger, or SMS. Pick the channel the prospect is most active on.

Write from your personal account, not an agency account. You are 18 and starting out -- own it.

---

### DM Version A: "I noticed something" (soft, observation-based)

> Hey [First Name] -- I came across [Business Name] on Google and noticed you've got some great work but no gallery on your site showing it. I build websites for landscapers that display project photos properly and show up on Google searches in [City]. Would you be open to seeing what I put together for a landscaping company similar to yours?

**Best for:** Landscapers with Instagram presence but no website portfolio. Keeps it specific and observational, not salesy.

---

### DM Version B: "Quick question" (direct, low-pressure)

> Hi [First Name], quick question -- is getting more estimate requests from homeowners something you're actively working on right now? I build websites for landscaping companies in [City] that rank on Google and book consultations automatically, including through an AI chatbot that responds after hours. Happy to show you a demo if that's something you're open to.

**Best for:** Cold prospects on Facebook or LinkedIn who have not interacted with you. Short. One ask.

---

### DM Version C: "Your work deserves a better home" (compliment-driven)

> Hey -- I've been following [Business Name] on Instagram and your project photos are legitimately impressive. The problem is they're buried on Instagram and not visible to homeowners searching Google for a landscaper in [City]. I build websites that give landscaping companies a proper portfolio with Google visibility and a lead form. Would a 10-minute call to show you an example be worth your time?

**Best for:** Instagram-active landscapers with good content. Opens with genuine compliment. The "10-minute call" ask is low commitment.

## 5. Personalized Prospect DMs

Five prospect profiles pulled from the niche decision. Each DM is written for that profile type. Swap in the real business name, owner name, and city when you deploy.

---

### Prospect Type 1: Maps Pack presence, no website or broken site

**Profile:** 20-80 Google reviews, ranking 4-10 in Maps Pack, no website or a one-page site with no portfolio. Clear gap between reputation and web presence.

**DM:**

> Hi [Name] -- I found [Business Name] on Google Maps while looking at landscaping companies in [City]. You've got solid reviews but your website doesn't have a portfolio showing your work, which is what homeowners look for before they call. I build websites for landscaping companies that fix exactly that -- a clean gallery of your projects, your Google reviews front and center, and a contact form that routes to your phone. I can show you a demo that looks similar to what I'd build for you. Worth 10 minutes?

**Why this works:** References their specific gap (no portfolio). The money math is implicit -- they have reviews, they just need a site to convert them.

---

### Prospect Type 2: Active Instagram, no website

**Profile:** Posts project photos 2-4x per week on Instagram or Facebook. Good content. No website or a basic site that doesn't capture the Instagram traffic.

**DM:**

> Hey [Name] -- I follow [Business Name] on Instagram and honestly your projects are great. Backyard transformations, hardscaping, the whole thing. But if someone sees your post and Googles "[Business Name] [City]" to book a consultation, what do they land on? I build websites that turn those Instagram viewers into actual estimate calls -- with a gallery that shows the same quality work you post, plus an AI chatbot that responds to inquiries while you're on a job. Can I send you a quick preview?

**Why this works:** Opens with a genuine compliment on specific content. The "what do they land on" question creates mild urgency without being pushy. Chatbot mention is the differentiator.

---

### Prospect Type 3: Nextdoor/Facebook group advertiser, no site

**Profile:** Runs posts or ads in neighborhood Facebook groups or on Nextdoor. Gets leads from word of mouth and community groups. No website to send warm prospects to.

**DM:**

> Hi [Name] -- I saw [Business Name] mentioned in [Group Name] and noticed you don't have a website linked. I build websites for landscaping companies so when someone from the neighborhood Googles you after seeing a recommendation, they land somewhere that actually converts them into a call -- portfolio, reviews, a contact form, the works. First-time setup is a one-time fee, no monthly retainer. Want me to send you an example of what it looks like?

**Why this works:** References their actual marketing behavior (Nextdoor/Facebook). The "one-time fee, no retainer" removes the recurring cost objection upfront, which community-based small operators are usually sensitive to.

---

### Prospect Type 4: Premium operator with generic template site

**Profile:** Higher pricing, named crews, equipment photos, branded uniforms. Positions as a boutique operator. Current site is a Wix/Squarespace template that doesn't match their premium positioning.

**DM:**

> Hey [Name] -- I was on the [Business Name] website and noticed it doesn't really match the level of work you do. Your Instagram shows premium installs but the site looks like a generic template. I build custom websites for landscaping companies that match a high-end positioning -- clean portfolio layout, Fraunces serif headings, dark professional color system, AI chatbot for after-hours leads. The kind of site a homeowner spending $10-15k on their yard expects to see before they pick up the phone. Can I show you a preview?

**Why this works:** Flatters their positioning while naming the gap precisely. "Homeowner spending $10-15k" validates their target customer and makes the site investment feel proportional.

---

### Prospect Type 5: Recent rebrand or expansion

**Profile:** Recently moved, expanded to a new city, added services, or rebranded. Old website still reflects the prior identity. A new site frames as a milestone, not a cold pitch.

**DM:**

> Hi [Name] -- I saw [Business Name] recently expanded to [New City / added new service]. Congrats on the growth. I noticed your website still reflects the old setup -- it doesn't list [New City] or [New Service], which means homeowners searching there aren't finding you. I build websites for landscaping companies and a "we've grown, the site needs to catch up" project is exactly what I do. Quick, clean, reflects where you are now. One-time fee, delivered in 5-7 days. Want to see an example?

**Why this works:** Frames the pitch as responding to their momentum, not their weakness. Removes the cold-pitch feeling. The business milestone makes the timing feel right.

## 6. Key Objection Responses

**"We already have a website."**

> That's great -- can I ask, does it have a gallery showing your actual projects? And do you know how it's ranking on Google for "[City] landscaping"? Most landscaping sites I look at have one or both of those gaps. Happy to take a quick look and tell you honestly what I see.

**"I don't have time for this right now."**

> Totally fair. I'll keep it simple -- I need your photos, your logo, your list of services, and your contact info. You send those over and I handle everything else in 5-7 days. No calls required beyond a quick review at the end.

**"How much does it cost?"**

> $1,500 one-time. No monthly retainer unless you want me to update it over time, which is $150/month. Most clients find that one booked job covers the cost of the site entirely.

**"We get our work from referrals, we don't need a website."**

> Referrals are great. The website makes the referral convert faster -- when someone hears about you from a neighbor and Googles your name, a good site is the difference between them booking a call and them finding your competitor instead. It also lets you charge what you're worth because the site backs up your quality.

---

## 7. Money Math (Use in Any Conversation)

> One new install job from the site pays for the site twice over. If the site generates 4 qualified inquiries a month and you close 1 install at $10,000, that's $10,000 in new revenue from a $1,500 investment. Add 5 new maintenance clients at $200/month and that's $12,000 a year on top of that.

Use this when a prospect objects to the price or says "I don't know if it's worth it."
