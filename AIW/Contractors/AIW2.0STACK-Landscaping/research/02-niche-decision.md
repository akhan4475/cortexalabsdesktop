## Niche Profile

**Target client:** Local landscaping or lawn care company, 1-20 employees, $200k-2M/year in residential work, weak or no website, losing leads to better-ranked competitors on Google Maps.

**Target sub-segment:** Design/install specialists (or companies that want to position that way), not pure maintenance shops. Higher ticket ($8-15k/project), not platform-threatened by LawnStarter or Lawn Love.

**End customer:** Homeowner or property owner making a considered purchase based on visual proof (portfolio), responsiveness, and confidence the crew will show up and do the job right.

## 5 Businesses to Approach This Week

Pulled from `01-agencies.md` competitive review and local niche knowledge. These are the profile types to target, not specific named companies -- find equivalents in Ayaan's local market (Maryland/DC area) via Google Maps.

1. A landscaping company with 20-80 Google reviews, no website or a site that does not show a portfolio, ranking position 4-10 in the Maps Pack for "[city] landscaping." This is the highest-priority prospect type. Clear gap, clear ROI story.

2. A design/install landscaper who has active Instagram or Facebook posts showing project photos but no website to drive that traffic to a lead form. The content already exists -- they just need a home for it.

3. A landscaping company that advertises on Nextdoor or neighborhood Facebook groups but has no website to send interested neighbors to. These owners already understand online visibility; they are pre-sold on the concept.

4. A lawn care company positioning itself as a premium or "boutique" operator (higher pricing, named crews, uniforms, equipment photos) but with a generic template website that does not reflect that premium positioning. Easy upgrade sale.

5. A landscaping company that recently moved, expanded service area, or rebranded but whose online presence still reflects the old identity. A website refresh frames as a business milestone, not a cold sales pitch.
