NICHE SYNTHESIS, landscaping

Score from Module 2A: 25/35

End customer in one sentence: A homeowner or property owner who wants their outdoor space improved -- through maintenance, redesign, or hardscaping -- and makes the decision based on visual proof (portfolio), responsiveness, and trust that the crew will show up and do it right.

The student's client in one sentence: A local landscaping or lawn care company with 1-20 employees, doing $200k-2M/year in residential work, with a weak or nonexistent website and losing business to competitors who rank better on Google Maps.

---

Top 5 Insights:

1. Design/install beats maintenance as the target segment. Maintenance is commoditized by LawnStarter and Lawn Love platforms. Design/install is not platform-threatened, has higher ticket sizes ($8-15k per project), and creates a client relationship that leads to ongoing work. Positioning the student's client as a design/install specialist -- even if they also do maintenance -- unlocks both higher-ticket conversions and platform differentiation.

2. Before/after portfolio is the primary conversion tool. This is a visual purchase. Customers decide based on seeing past work, not reading copy. An 8-16 photo gallery of named local projects is more valuable than any headline or copy block on the page.

3. Responsiveness is a genuine differentiator. Multiple reviews explicitly praise same-day response, communication, and showing up on time. These are so consistently praised that they must be the norm to fail on and the exception to deliver on. The website should make responsiveness a core promise, not just imply it.

4. Platform fragmentation is low. Unlike roofing (where large regional players exist), most landscaping companies are tiny and unsophisticated online. A new client with a strong website + GMB listing can rank in the Maps Pack faster than in roofing.

5. Cost transparency content captures planning-phase buyers. SERP shows high demand for "how much does landscaping cost" queries. A client whose website publishes honest cost ranges captures early-funnel traffic that competitors ignore. This is an easy content win the student can deliver as part of the site.

---

Top 3 Risks if We Pick This Niche:

- Platform competition in the maintenance segment. LawnStarter and Lawn Love dominate "lawn care near me." The student must steer clients toward design/install positioning to avoid competing with these platforms directly.
- Longer sales cycle for design/install. Unlike roofing (urgency-driven) or pool cleaning (recurring/subscription), landscape design is a considered purchase that can take weeks from first visit to signed contract.
- Seasonal variance. Spring and summer dominate. Fall can be strong (cleanup). Winter is slow in most markets. Sun Belt markets (Phoenix, Dallas, Atlanta) reduce this.

---

Top 3 Reasons to Pick This Niche:

- High fragmentation = low competition for a new entrant. Most landscape companies have terrible websites. The bar is low and easy to clear.
- Strong recurring revenue potential. Maintenance clients become long-term relationships. A website that converts one maintenance client generates $3,600-6,000/year in recurring revenue for the client -- a strong ROI story.
- Ayaan has no direct experience but the niche is learnable quickly. The customer voice is clear, the trust signals are visual (portfolio-first), and the website template is highly standardized across all landscaping sub-types.

---

Money math headline: "If your website generates 4 qualified consultations a month and you close 1 install job, that's $10,000 in new revenue. Add 5 new recurring maintenance clients and that's $18,000-30,000 per year from one website."
