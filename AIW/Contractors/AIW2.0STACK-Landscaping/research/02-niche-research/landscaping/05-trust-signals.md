## Top 5 Trust Signals (Priority Order)

### 1. Before/After Portfolio with Named Local Projects
**Justification:** Landscaping is a visual purchase. Multiple reviews reference the quality of completed work as the reason for hiring and rehiring. "Sal recommended design changes that were better than anything I could have thought of myself" shows that end customers need to see proof of design quality before they trust the company with their yard. Named local projects ("Backyard transformation, Plano TX, 2024") are more credible than unnamed portfolio items.

**What to build:** Gallery with 8-16 before/after pairs. Each labeled with city/neighborhood + service type. No stock images.

### 2. Google Review Count + Recent Star Rating
**Justification:** Same pattern as roofing. Homeowners confirm choices through reviews. High review volume + recency = trust before contact. The landscaping market has slightly lower review volume per business than roofing, so 30-50 reviews with recent entries is competitive.

**What to build:** Review count + star rating in hero. Embedded Google reviews section mid-page.

### 3. Licensed + Insured Badge with State License
**Justification:** Landscaping work (especially hardscaping, irrigation, tree removal) carries property damage and liability risk. "Licensed and insured" is expected, but showing the actual license number elevates trust above competitors who just claim it.

**What to build:** "Licensed & Insured" badge with state contractor license number.

### 4. Service Area Map or Neighborhood List
**Justification:** Landscaping companies serve tight geographic areas. Customers check service coverage before they read anything. An explicit list of cities and neighborhoods served signals "we know your area" and pre-qualifies the lead for the crew.

**What to build:** Explicit city/neighborhood list or embedded map showing service radius. Include named neighborhoods in larger cities.

### 5. Design Expertise Signal (Consultation Offer + Named Designer)
**Justification:** Multiple reviews praise design recommendations beyond what the customer requested. Customers are paying for expertise, not just labor. A named "Lead Designer" or "Design Consultation" offer signals a higher-tier service than competitors who just show up and cut grass.

**What to build:** "Free Design Consultation" CTA (not just "Free Estimate"). A brief bio for the lead designer or owner. This differentiates from commodity lawn mowing services.

## Niche-Specific Certifications and Badges

| Badge | What it signals | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| NALP Member (National Assoc. of Landscape Professionals) | Industry standards, training | Medium |
| ISA Certified Arborist | Tree expertise, safety | Medium (if trees offered) |
| Licensed Irrigation Contractor | State license for irrigation | High (if irrigation offered) |
| BBB Accredited | Consumer trust | Medium |
| Angi Verified | Consumer platform | Low-medium |
| Google Guaranteed | Background-checked | Medium |

---

## Press / "As Seen In" Expectations
Not expected. Local landscapers do not have press coverage. Skip unless genuine.

---

## Photo/Video Testimonial Expectations
Before/after photos outperform video testimonials in this niche. If the client has drone footage of a completed project, that is a high-converting asset. Short "walkthrough" videos of completed yards are more useful than talking-head testimonials.
