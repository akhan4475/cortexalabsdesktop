# 04 - CRO Patterns: Landscaping

Source: `raw/agencies-search.json`, `raw/serp-rankings.json`, `raw/maps-reviews.json`. No CRO crawl screenshots. [inferred from cross-source analysis]

---

## Most Common Section Sequence

1. **Hero** -- visual background (drone shot of completed yard or lush garden), headline + primary CTA
2. **Social proof bar** -- star rating, review count, years in business
3. **Services grid** -- lawn maintenance, landscape design, irrigation, hardscaping, seasonal cleanup
4. **Portfolio / Before & After gallery** -- most critical section in this niche (visual purchase)
5. **Why us** -- 3-4 differentiators (responsive, local, licensed, on time)
6. **Review section** -- Google reviews or embedded testimonials
7. **Service areas** -- city/neighborhood list (local SEO anchor)
8. **Process steps** -- "How it works" (consultation, design, install/maintain)
9. **Final CTA** -- estimate or consultation form

---

## Hero Composition

- **Imagery:** Completed yard transformations (before/after), lush green lawns, hardscaping details. Drone overhead shots of large properties are high-converting in the design/install segment. Crew-in-action photos work for maintenance.
- **Primary CTA:** "Get a Free Estimate" or "Schedule a Consultation" -- design-first language converts better than generic "Contact Us."
- **Secondary CTA:** Gallery link ("See Our Work") -- visual buyers want proof before they call.
- **Social proof placement:** Review count and star rating in hero or sticky header. Google logo carries weight.

---

## Key Difference from Roofing

Landscaping is an **aspirational** purchase, not an urgent one (unless irrigation emergency). Roofing CRO is fear-driven (stop the leak, fix the storm damage). Landscaping CRO is vision-driven (imagine your yard transformed). This changes the hero: imagery and before/after weight heavier in landscaping than in roofing.

---

## Trust Stack Ordering

1. Before/after gallery with named projects (most important -- visual validation)
2. Google review count + star rating
3. Years in business / local tenure
4. Licensed and insured badge
5. Service area list (local specificity)
6. Design consultation offer (signals expertise, not just labor)

---

## Form Patterns

- **Primary form:** Name, phone, service type (dropdown: maintenance / design / irrigation / hardscaping), address or zip code.
- **Address/zip:** Important in landscaping -- crew scheduling is geographic. Zip code pre-qualifies the lead.
- **Click-to-call:** Secondary CTA on mobile. Landscaping owners often prefer phone calls for initial quotes.
- **Gallery-first flow [inferred]:** High-converting landscaping sites often route users through portfolio first, then into a form. "Saw work you like? Let's do yours."

---

## Sticky Elements

- Sticky header with phone + "Free Estimate" CTA.
- Mobile: Fixed bottom bar with call button.
- Optional: "View Portfolio" shortcut in sticky nav for visual-first buyers.

---

## Divergence from Universal Converting Wireframe

Portfolio/gallery section moves up to position 4 (before "Why us") in high-converting landscaping sites. In a visual purchase, proof comes before pitch. Also: service area section is more important here than in most niches -- landscapers serve tight geographic radiuses, and customers want to know "do you serve my neighborhood" before reading anything else.
