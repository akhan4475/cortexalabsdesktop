## Top 5 Trust Signals (Priority Order)

### 1. Before/After Photo Documentation Per Visit
**Justification:** "Before and after pictures = peace of mind" is a verbatim quote from a 5-star review. This is not just a nice-to-have -- it is an explicit trust signal that converts recurring customers. Gutter cleaning especially benefits from this because homeowners cannot see the inside of their gutters. Photographic proof that work was done is a direct anxiety remover. [Frequency: 3 mentions, Intensity: "peace of mind" language, Addressability: high -- photos can be shown on the website as a feature]

**What to build:** A section explaining "Every visit is photo-documented. We send before/after images after each service." Show example before/after pairs on the website.

### 2. Google Review Count + Recent Star Rating
**Justification:** "Finding a reliable gutter cleaner can be hit or miss" -- the industry reputation problem means reviews are the primary trust filter before booking. High review count + recency signals that the company is consistent and active.

**What to build:** Review count + stars in hero. Embedded Google reviews section.

### 3. Transparent Pricing (No Hidden Fees)
**Justification:** "Fair and transparent with no hidden fees" praised in multiple reviews. The inverse (hidden fees, surprise invoices) is an active fear. Unlike roofing where pricing varies too much to publish, pool and gutter cleaning is standardized enough that showing price ranges is feasible and trust-building.

**What to build:** Pricing section or table showing base rates (e.g., "Gutter cleaning from $X for single-story, $X for two-story"). "No hidden fees" stated explicitly, not just implied.

### 4. Insured + Background-Checked Crew
**Justification:** Gutter cleaning requires ladder work and roof access in the homeowner's absence. Pool cleaning involves chemical handling and access to the property. "Worked safely" is praised in reviews. Customers are inviting a stranger onto their property. Insurance + background checks remove the primary safety fear.

**What to build:** "Fully insured and background-checked" badge. State contractor license if applicable.

### 5. Satisfaction Guarantee / Re-Service Promise
**Justification:** "Solid performance" and "will continue using" reviews show long-term retention. A re-service guarantee ("if you're not happy, we come back free") reduces first-booking risk and is a differentiator from the "hit or miss" competition.

**What to build:** Explicit guarantee statement on the site. "Not satisfied? We come back, no charge."

## Press / "As Seen In" Expectations
Not expected. Small local operators. Skip.

---

## Photo/Video Testimonial Expectations
Before/after job photos outperform video testimonials. Short video walkthroughs (30-45 seconds showing a clean pool or cleared gutters post-service) convert well as social media content but are not expected on the website. Written reviews with star ratings are sufficient.
