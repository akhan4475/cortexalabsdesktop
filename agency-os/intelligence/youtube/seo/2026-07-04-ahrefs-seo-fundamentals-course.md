## Key Tactics

1. **Traffic potential > search volume** — Search volume is misleading. The top-ranking page for a keyword ranks for hundreds of other keywords too. Traffic potential = how much actual traffic the #1 ranking page gets. Use Ahrefs SERP Checker to see real traffic numbers. A keyword with 300 searches/month might actually drive 5,000 visits/month to the top page.
2. **Five-point keyword checklist** — Every keyword must pass: (1) search demand exists, (2) traffic potential is real, (3) business potential (1–3 score), (4) search intent is matchable, (5) ranking difficulty is achievable. Metrics alone don't decide — context + judgment determines what to target.
3. **Business potential scoring (1–3)** — Score every keyword by how easily you can convert the traffic. Score 3: transactional, direct product/service fit. Score 2: commercial, can recommend your service naturally. Score 1: informational, builds audience but weak conversion. Score 0: irrelevant — skip it. This stops you from wasting time on high-traffic, zero-conversion keywords.
4. **Three C's of search intent** — To match intent for any keyword: (1) Content Type (blog post, product page, video, landing page), (2) Content Format (how-to, listicle, comparison, opinion), (3) Content Angle (freshness, best, cheapest, for beginners). Analyze top 5 Google results for all three C's before writing a single word.
5. **Ranking difficulty analysis — 4 questions** — Before targeting a keyword: (1) Do any top pages fail to match intent? (2) Can I get more quality backlinks than the top pages? (3) Is my domain rating in a similar range? (4) Is my site topically authoritative on this subject? The more "yes" answers, the better your ranking chances.
6. **Topical authority outranks domain authority** — A DR42 single-topic site consistently outranks DR90 general sites on their topic. Topical authority = comprehensive coverage of one subject area. For contractors: a pool contractor site that covers everything pool-related (installation, cleaning, repair, chemicals, equipment) beats a general home services site on pool queries.
7. **Competitor-led content research** — Before writing, open top 3 ranking pages for your target keyword. Look for: (a) subtopics they all cover = mandatory inclusions, (b) what they missed = your differentiation, (c) common keywords they all rank for (content gap analysis). Ahrefs Content Gap tool automates this — paste 3 competitor URLs, get keyword gaps instantly.
8. **Internal linking = highest effort-to-value ratio** — Internal links pass authority between pages, help Google understand site structure, and keep users engaged. For every new page: link to it from 2+ related existing pages, and link from it to your most important pages. Use Google (`site:yourdomain.com + topic`) to find internal link opportunities.
9. **Keyword modifiers to filter by intent** — Use modifier lists to batch-filter keywords by intent type. Commercial modifiers: "best," "top," "vs," "review." Informational modifiers: "how," "what," "why," "guide," "tutorial." Apply these as filters in keyword tools to instantly separate buyer-intent from research-intent keywords.
10. **Organic competitor analysis for keyword discovery** — Your organic competitors ≠ business competitors. They're sites ranking for your target keywords. Find them via Ahrefs "traffic share by domain" report. Check their "top pages" to find high-traffic topics they cover that aren't in your keyword list. Repeat for 3–5 competitors = unlimited content ideas.
11. **On-page SEO is about intent match, not keyword density** — Google doesn't rank pages for hitting a keyword 10 times. It ranks pages for comprehensively satisfying the searcher. Evidence: Healthline ranks #1 for "diet plan" with one mention of the phrase; GQ ranks for "classiest watch" with zero instances of "classiest." Content depth and intent match > keyword stuffing.
12. **Meta descriptions: write them, but don't obsess** — Google rewrites meta descriptions 63% of the time. Still worth writing because they influence click-through rate when Google does use them. Rule: 150–160 chars, action-oriented, includes keyword, reads like ad copy. Spend 5 minutes, not 30.
13. **Image optimization: three-part process** — (1) Descriptive file name (pool-cleaning-phoenix.jpg, not IMG_001.jpg), (2) Alt text that describes the image for someone who can't see it (also targets image search), (3) Compressed file sizes (WebP format). Image search traffic is real and accumulated over time.
14. **URL slugs: short + descriptive + keyword-included** — Use `/pool-contractor-phoenix` not `/page?id=12847`. Short descriptive slugs help users understand the page before clicking and help Google categorize content. No need to force exact-match phrases — close variants are fine.
15. **Link building for new sites: start with what you already know** — Step 1: create content worth linking to (comprehensive guides, original data, free tools). Step 2: journalist query platforms (HARO successor: Source of Sources, free). Step 3: resource/linkable assets. Don't start cold outreach until you have 10–15 solid pieces of content — you need something worth linking to first.

## Frameworks

### Five-Point Keyword Evaluation Checklist
```
1. SEARCH DEMAND: Min search volume filter (≥300 for moderate traffic)
2. TRAFFIC POTENTIAL: Check top-ranking page's actual traffic (not just volume)
3. BUSINESS POTENTIAL: Score 0-3 — how easily can you convert this traffic?
   3 = direct purchase/hire intent
   2 = comparison stage, easy product/service mention
   1 = informational, builds audience only
   0 = skip
4. INTENT MATCH: Can you create the content type the top results show?
   (e.g., if top results are e-commerce pages and you have a blog, skip it)
5. RANKING DIFFICULTY: 4-question analysis (see below)

Target keywords that pass all 5 checks.
```

### Ranking Difficulty — 4-Question Analysis
```
For each target keyword, check the SERP and answer:

Q1: Do top pages fail to closely match search intent?
    YES → opportunity (you can match it better)

Q2: Can I build more/better backlinks than the top pages?
    YES → advantage

Q3: Is my domain rating in a similar range as top sites?
    YES → competitive

Q4: Is my site topically authoritative on this subject?
    YES → advantage

More YES answers = better ranking chances.
All NO = don't target yet, build up first.
```

### Three C's of Search Intent
```
CONTENT TYPE: blog post | product page | video | landing page
  → Look at dominant type in top 5 results

CONTENT FORMAT (for blog/landing pages):
  → How-to / tutorial / listicle / comparison / opinion / tool

CONTENT ANGLE:
  → Freshness ("2026 update"), Best, Cheapest, For beginners, For pros

Apply all three before writing. Example:
Keyword: "best pool cleaners"
  → Type: blog post
  → Format: listicle (multiple recommendations)
  → Angle: freshness (include current year) + value (best for the money)
```

### Content Research Process (Competitor-Led)
```
Step 1: Google your target keyword
Step 2: Open top 3–5 relevant results (exclude mismatched intent outliers)
Step 3: Read all of them fully — note:
  (a) Topics every page covers = table stakes (must include)
  (b) Topics only 1–2 cover = strong signal (include for differentiation)
  (c) Topics none cover = gap (your competitive advantage)
Step 4: Run Ahrefs Content Gap or similar tool — paste 3 competitor URLs
Step 5: Filter for keywords where 2–3 of the pages rank in top 10
Step 6: Add subtopics from gap keywords to your outline

Output: outline that covers table stakes + fills gaps = outranks all three.
```

### Internal Linking Strategy
```
For every new page published:
  → Find 2+ existing pages on related topics
  → Add contextual link in body copy to new page
  → From new page: link to money pages and related cluster posts

To find opportunities:
  → Google: site:yourdomain.com [topic of new page]
  → Visit related pages → find natural insertion point
  → Add anchor text that describes the destination page topic
```

## How to Apply — CortexaLabs

**Keyword research workflow for contractor SEO upsell:**
1. Seed keywords: pool contractor [city], roofing contractor [city], etc.
2. Phrase match report in Ahrefs or SEMrush → filter volume ≥100
3. Apply business potential scoring: "pool leak repair [city]" = 3, "what is pool shock" = 1
4. Three C's check on top results for each shortlisted keyword
5. Ranking difficulty — 4 questions — to confirm it's winnable

**On-page execution checklist per page:**
- Match content type from top results (don't write a blog post if top results are service pages)
- Research top 3 competitors for the keyword before writing
- Include all subtopics covered by 2+ top pages
- Add your differentiation (client results, local knowledge, specific data)
- Short keyword-included URL slug
- Internal link from 2+ related pages

**Link building for early-stage sites:**
- Priority 1: Create content worth linking to (contractor guides, cost calculators, checklists)
- Priority 2: Source of Sources — 15 min/day, free, journalist queries
- Priority 3: Local citations (contractor directories, local business listings)
- Don't start aggressive outreach until 10–15 solid content pieces published

**For contractor clients specifically:**
- GMB is the #1 local SEO action (map pack for "[niche] near me")
- Start keyword research with "[service] [city]" transactional queries — these are money pages
- Build topical authority on local: "best pool contractor in [city]," "[city] pool installation guide," "[city] pool maintenance tips"
