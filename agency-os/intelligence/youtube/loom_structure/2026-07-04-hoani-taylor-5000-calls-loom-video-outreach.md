## Key Tactics

1. **Video = trust shortcut, not a sales pitch** — The entire purpose of the Loom is to build trust, not sell. High-ticket ($5k+) requires trust. Cold outbound starts at zero trust. Video is the richest medium you can use at scale. Don't try to sell in the video.
2. **Trust ladder: text < voice < video < face-to-face** — The richer the medium, the easier trust builds. Video lets you show face, tone, mannerisms — which text cannot convey. This is WHY Loom outperforms written DMs for higher-ticket offers.
3. **Permission-based Loom outperforms cold Loom** — Send a text message first: "I made a quick video showing you X. Mind if I send it over?" If they say yes, THEN make and send the Loom. Result: 5–10 videos per booked call (vs much worse cold). Only people who want it watch it — reply rate, watch rate, and call rate all dramatically higher.
4. **Not everyone deserves a video** — Only send the Loom to people who've raised their hand (replied positively to initial outreach). Sending Looms cold is inefficient.
5. **Permission-based email template for Loom** — "Hey [first name]. We helped similar [niche] companies in [city] generate more [specific outcome]. I made a video showing how this might work for you. Do you mind if I send it over?"
6. **Loom script principle: naturalness > precision** — Don't follow a script rigidly — if scripted delivery feels robotic, go without one. The goal is GENUINE, not polished. "Hey, I know it's a bit odd to get a video like this, but I think this will be interesting for you" is better than a rehearsed pitch.
7. **After positive reply: keep ball in your court** — Don't send a booking link. Propose specific times directly. Send call attempt first. If they don't pick up: text + email + "I put us in for 4pm Friday — let me know if that doesn't work." This removes friction and shows initiative.
8. **Reconfirmation before the call** — Ask them to confirm the call. If no reconfirmation by morning of the call: manual follow-up ("just confirming we're still on for 4pm today?"). Reduces no-shows dramatically.
9. **Expand communication channels after reply** — If they reply positively on email: connect on LinkedIn. Opens more channels for follow-up so you're not dependent on one platform.
10. **Automated Loom variant (scale)** — Record one video with audio. Software overlays your audio onto each prospect's website while scrolling it. Each prospect gets a personalized-looking video. No AI voice/face — keep it human. Hoani's team sends 200k of these/month across 40 businesses.

## Frameworks

### Trust Acquisition Sequence (Loom-based)
```
Initial cold outreach (DM or email)
    ↓
[Permission request] "I made a quick video for you on X — mind if I send it over?"
    ↓
[If yes] Record/send personalized Loom
    ↓
[Nurture positive reply] — Call attempt → voicemail → text + email with proposed time
    ↓
[Book discovery call with proposed times, no Calendly link]
    ↓
[Pre-call] Reconfirm booking morning of
    ↓
[Day of] Manual follow-up if no reconfirm
    ↓
Discovery call → Demo call → Close
    ↓
Fulfillment → Testimonial → Referral
```

### Loom Video Script Structure (Permission-Based)
```
[0:00 – 0:15] Acknowledge the oddity + establish realness
"Hey [first name], Ayaan here from CortexaLabs. I know it's a bit odd to get a video like this — but I genuinely think there's something worth seeing here for your business."

[0:15 – 1:00] Show their current situation (screen share their website)
"So I pulled up [business name]'s website. Here's what I noticed right away..."
[point out 2-3 specific issues: no clear CTA, no trust signals, not mobile-friendly etc.]

[1:00 – 2:00] Paint the opportunity (without selling)
"Contractors in [city] who have sites that do X, Y, Z are the ones that tend to get the inbound calls. Here's what that looks like..."
[show an example or describe the gap]

[2:00 – 2:30] Simple human CTA
"I'd love to show you what this could look like specifically for [business name]. If you're open to a quick 20-min call, just reply and we can find a time."
[Don't say "book now" or push Calendly — invite a reply]
```

### Lead Nurture After Positive Reply
```
Prospect replies positively
    ↓
Attempt phone call immediately
    ↓ (if no answer)
Send text: "Hey [name], tried to call — sounds like we should chat. I've blocked out Thursday at 3pm or Friday at 11am — which works better?"
    ↓ (confirmation received)
Book in your calendar. Send invite.
    ↓
Day before: "Just confirming we're still on for [time] tomorrow?"
    ↓ (no reply to confirmation)
Day of: "Hey — we're on for [time] today. Talk soon."
```

## Exact Phrases Worth Testing

> "Hey, I know it's a bit odd to get a video like this, but I have something that I think will be really interesting for you. I made the effort to make a video, so hopefully you watch this and feel genuine."
> Context: Loom video opener. Works because it pre-empts skepticism and establishes authenticity.

> "We helped similar [niche] companies in [city] generate more [specific outcome]. I made a video showing how this might work for you. Do you mind if I send it over?"
> Context: Permission-based Loom request (email or DM). Specific niche + city + outcome = personalized feel without manual effort.

> "I tried to call you but couldn't get through. I've put us in for a call at [time] on [day] — let me know if that doesn't work."
> Context: Post-reply follow-up. Keeps ball in your court. Doesn't wait for them to book — assumes forward motion.

## How to Apply

**CortexaLabs Loom Permission Request (IG DM or email):**
"Hey [name] — I noticed [business name]'s website and had an idea for how it could be bringing you more roofing/pool/landscaping jobs from your area. Made a quick 2-min video showing exactly what I mean. Mind if I send it over?"

**CortexaLabs Loom Video Flow (screen-share their site):**
1. Acknowledge + establish real: "Hey [name], Ayaan from CortexaLabs — bit of an unusual message but I think you'll find this genuinely useful"
2. Pull up their site live: "So here's what [business name]'s website looks like right now..."
3. Point out 2-3 specific gaps (no CTA, no reviews widget, not mobile-optimized, slow load, no before/after work shown)
4. Paint the vision: "Contractors who have sites that lead with their work photos, a clear call button, and their Google reviews front-and-center — they're the ones getting called first"
5. CTA: "Happy to show you what that could look like for your business specifically. Just reply and we'll find 20 minutes"

**Do not send Calendly link in the Loom.** Invite a reply. Then propose specific times. Then book.
