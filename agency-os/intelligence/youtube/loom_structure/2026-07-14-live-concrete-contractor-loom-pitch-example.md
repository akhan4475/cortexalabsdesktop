## Key Tactics

1. **Frame the site as a ranking/lead-gen asset from the first line, not a design deliverable.** Opens by explicitly separating "visually appealing" from the actual purpose: "The websites are not just meant to be visually appealing, they're really meant to help you rank on Google and bring in organic leads — people already searching for [stamped concrete / driveways / patios]." Sets the frame before showing anything.
2. **Google My Business gap called out immediately, offered as a value-add.** "I noticed you didn't have a Google My Business — I might be mistaken, but if you don't, I can help you set up a Google My Business, help you get reviews on there." This is offered as part of the pitch, not the paid tier — positions the presenter as solving the whole online-presence problem, not just building a page.
3. **Personalization goes beyond the site — references what's in their actual store.** "I've seen right now in your store you do epoxy as well, so we can add all of your services onto it." Shows research beyond just looking at their current website — implies checking their physical storefront/other listings too.
4. **Lead notification mechanic explained concretely, not just claimed.** "As soon as someone fills out this form you get a notification via SMS and email — full name, phone, email address, and what they said." Specific fields named, both channels named — makes an abstract "you'll get leads" claim feel like a real, working system.
5. **Before/after scroller framed against a specific buyer psychology, not just "cool feature."** Explicitly ties it to first-time buyers: "Some people are like, well, this is my first time getting a patio done and I want to see some previous work." Names the objection (no track record visible) before showing the feature that solves it.
6. **Instagram feed embedded directly on the site, with a live scroll-through and a "follow us" / "view story" action that pushes traffic back to Instagram.** Presenter explicitly says "that's where I found you" — turns the prospect's own existing content into personalization proof AND cross-promotes their IG from the new site. Not mentioned in any prior captured source — a genuinely new structural idea.
7. **CTA (the contact form) is referenced repeatedly throughout the walkthrough, not just once at the end** — mentioned near the top, again mid-video, and again near the close, each time re-anchored to a specific section of the page.
8. **Explicit "not just tire kickers" line** — "I help contractors stay booked out getting consistently quality leads, not just tire kickers." Pre-empts the unstated objection that inbound leads are often low-quality.
9. **Audience-aware insight on why GMB/Google presence matters even when a business already does well on Instagram:** "if it's my first time searching, especially for the elderly people who don't use a lot of social media, they typically go to Google." Ties the GMB/reviews pitch to a specific buyer segment the business might be missing via Instagram alone.
10. **Price is never stated in the video — explicitly deferred to a phone call.** "Prices can vary, we can talk over the phone — that way I get to know a little more about your business, I can give you a solid price and we can go on from there." No range given, no anchor — the call is framed as necessary to get an accurate number, not as a sales gate.
11. **Delivery style is genuinely unscripted** — run-on sentences, repeated words, "boom boom boom," self-corrections mid-sentence. This is rougher than the tightly-scripted example lines already in `skills/loom-personalization/SKILL.md` and still reportedly worked (it's a real send). Useful as calibration: the existing skill file's model scripts may be too polished/tight relative to what actually gets sent and works in practice.

## Frameworks

### Structure Observed (as actually delivered, not idealized)
```
1. Open — explain purpose: "not just visually appealing, meant to rank and bring organic leads"
2. Call out GMB gap, offer to set it up
3. Walk services section — tailored, catches an extra service from their store (epoxy)
4. Explain lead notification mechanic (SMS + email, specific fields)
5. Individual service pages — mention before/after scroller ties to first-timer trust
6. Project gallery (separate from before/after scroller)
7. Instagram feed embed + follow/story button — "that's where I found you"
8. Repeat CTA (contact form) reference
9. Service areas / quick links
10. Close on GMB + reviews + Yelp as "foundations" — audience-aware (elderly/non-social searchers)
11. Quality-lead framing ("not just tire kickers")
12. Defer price to a phone call — no number given in video
```
