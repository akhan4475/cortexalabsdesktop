## Key Tactics

1. **Phase structure (Day 1-90):** Phase 1 (Day 1-14) = setup only, no outreach yet. Phase 2 (Day 15-45) = first client acquisition. Phase 3 (Day 46-60) = delivery while continuing outreach in parallel. Phase 4 (Day 61-90) = stacking (1 new client/week + 1 upsell conversation/week).
2. **Phase 2 daily loop:** find 20 businesses/day on Google Maps with bad/no site → build a quick preview site for the strongest candidates using the template → record a 90-second Loom walking through it → send outreach message with the Loom link → follow up if no reply within 48 hours.
3. **Discovery call = 5 fixed questions:** What do you do? How are you currently getting customers? Do you have a website right now? Is it bringing in enough calls? Are you looking to grow this year? Listen for pain, then present the solution.
4. **Objection framework: Agree → Clarify → Reframe.** Example for "too expensive": Agree ("I completely understand"), Clarify/Reframe via question ("can I ask what is one new customer worth to your business?"). If a roofer says one job is worth $10,000 against a $1,000 fee, the objection resolves itself.
5. **Upsell script (after 30 days live):** "Now that your website's live, the next step is making sure people are able to find it easily. I have an SEO package that gets you ranking on Google Maps so customers in your area find you first. It's $400/mo and most of our clients see results within 60-90 days. Want me to get that started for you?" — converts a $99/mo client to a $499/mo client.
6. **Explicit rule: never stop outreach during delivery.** Delivery work is not a break from prospecting — both run in parallel the whole time.
7. **Revenue math to $10K (his stated model):** 10 sites × $1,000 = $10,000 build revenue. Same 10 clients × $99/mo hosting = $990/mo recurring. 3 clients on SEO × $400/mo = $1,200/mo recurring. Total: $10K+ collected with $1,500-2,000/mo recurring, all from 10 fully-served clients.

## Frameworks

### Phase KPI Benchmarks (creator's stated targets — useful as a comparison baseline, not a directive)
```
Phase 1 (Day 1-14):  CRM live, template built, workspace live, scripts written, niche confirmed
Phase 2 (Day 15-45): 20 outreach msgs/day, 5 Looms/week, 3 discovery calls/week, first client by day 45, $500 collected upfront
Phase 3 (Day 46-60): site delivered within 10 days of onboarding form, $500 collected on delivery, 2nd client by day 60
Phase 4 (Day 61-90): 1 new client/week, 1 upsell conversation/week, 10 total clients by day 90, $10K collected, $1,500-2,000/mo recurring
```

### Agree-Clarify-Reframe (objection handling)
```
1. Agree — validate the objection, don't argue
   "I completely understand..."
2. Clarify — ask a question that surfaces the real economics
   "...can I ask what is one new customer worth to your business?"
3. Reframe — let their own number do the work
   [Their job value] vs. [your fee] makes the fee look small by comparison
```

### Delivery Process (Day 1-10 after close)
```
Day 1-2:  onboarding form + asset collection (business name, services, photos, colors, contact info)
Day 3-5:  build full site from template + assets
Day 5-7:  send preview link, request honest feedback
Day 7-10: revisions to client's liking, collect remaining balance
```

## Exact Phrases Worth Testing

> "I completely understand, but can I ask what is one new customer worth to your business?"
> Context: price objection response. Works because it forces the prospect to state their own job value, which does the reframing for you.

> "Now that your website's live, the next step is making sure people are able to find it easily... Want me to get that started for you?"
> Context: SEO upsell opener, timed at 30 days post-launch, not at close.
