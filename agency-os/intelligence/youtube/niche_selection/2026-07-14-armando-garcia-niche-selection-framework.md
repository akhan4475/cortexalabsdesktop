## Key Tactics

1. **Niche = who do you help, nothing more.** A niche is the specific type of business you serve. It makes outreach messaging, templates, offer framing, and pricing all easier because everything is written for one specific reader instead of "businesses in general."
2. **The 3-question filter (10 minutes, before committing to anything):**
   - Can they afford $99/mo without it hurting? (jobs need to be worth thousands)
   - Do they need new customers every single week, not occasionally?
   - When searched on Google Maps, are most of their websites bad, broken, or missing?
   All three "yes" = valid niche.
3. **6 traits of a great niche:** need leads to survive, take high-value jobs, have bad current websites, are easy to find (Maps/social), exist nationwide (scalable across cities with the same scripts/templates), already understand they need a website (no education required).
4. **20-minute validation method:**
   - Pull top 20 Google Maps results in niche + city → 10+ should have bad/missing sites
   - Check average job value → target where one customer is worth ~10x the monthly fee
   - Repeat search across 5 different cities → 20+ prospects per city = nationally scalable
   - Check Instagram/Facebook bios for good-following-but-no-website-link accounts = hot prospects
5. **Restaurants are the explicit anti-niche** — low margins, constant menu/revision demands, can't sustain a retainer. Stay in local trade/service businesses.
6. **Niche choice is not permanent** — commit 60 days minimum, then reassess. The failure mode isn't picking wrong, it's not picking at all (analysis paralysis framed as "research").

## Frameworks

### 3-Question Niche Test
```
1. Can they afford $99/mo without hurting their pocket?
2. Do they need new customers every single week?
3. Are most of their Google Maps listings' websites bad/broken/missing?
→ All 3 yes = commit to this niche now.
```
