## Key Tactics

1. **Skip the paragraph — ask one short question** — Initial DM should not be a pitch, an explanation, or even an ask for interest. Just a single question the prospect can answer in 2 words. The shorter the ask, the more replies.
2. **Don't personalize the first message (contrary to guru advice)** — No need to reference specific posts, business details, or compliments. The audience (contractors, local businesses) is skeptical and direct. They want to know if you can help them, not be flattered.
3. **Ask about their biggest pain, not about your offer** — Initial question is about *them*: "Are you getting enough [desired outcome] right now?" Not "are you interested in our service?" This disarms sales resistance.
4. **Convert no-replies and nos into conversations** — One prospect said "I've tried these services before." Alicia responded by showing curiosity ("that's weird, what happened?"), which opened a 10-exchange back-and-forth that ended in a booked call. A "no" is just the start of a conversation.
5. **Know when to go for the call** — Don't let rapport-building drag. After ~5 exchanges where trust is building: "If you're open to having a quick call about this, when are you free?" Don't wait for permission to ask.
6. **Understand your niche psychology before writing any copy** — Contractors are: no-BS, skeptical, direct, busy. They want to know "how can you help me" not "here's why we're great." Every word should reflect that. Copy written for wrong psychology = no replies.
7. **5-of-7 opened messages responded with interest** — Raw conversion from seen-to-interest: ~71%. Most DMs are never opened. Getting past the "seen" stage is the real filter.
8. **Emails work with the same question-based opener** — The same approach (short question, no personalization) transfers to email outreach, not just DMs.

## Frameworks

### Question-Based Cold DM Structure
```
[Short greeting — first name only]
[One-line curiosity question about their situation / pain]
[Stop. Wait for reply.]

On reply → [Acknowledge + show genuine interest in their situation]
On "no" → [Acknowledge + ask curiosity question about WHY it didn't work]
After 5 exchanges → [Propose call with specific time]
```

### Psychology Map: Contractors
- **What they hate**: jargon, promises, pitches from strangers
- **What they respond to**: directness, practicality, evidence they're talking to someone real
- **Their core fear**: wasting money on marketing that doesn't work (been burned before)
- **Their core desire**: more jobs, less chasing, consistent pipeline
- **How they like to communicate**: short messages, fast, get to the point

### Conversation Recovery (the "no" flip)
```
Prospect: "I've tried these types of services before. Not interested."
Response: "That's interesting — what didn't work about it?"
[Prospect explains]
Response: "Okay, I get that. What are you currently doing for [marketing/leads]?"
[Continue natural back-and-forth]
After trust built → "If you're open to a quick call about fixing that, when works for you?"
```

## Exact Phrases Worth Testing

> "Hi [name], just a quick one — are you getting enough [outcome] right now?"
> Context: Cold opener. Replace [outcome] with "jobs from your site", "inbound leads", "website enquiries". Contractor version: "Hi [name], quick question — is your website actually bringing you jobs right now?"

> "That's interesting — what didn't work about it?"
> Context: Responding to "I've tried this before / not interested." Opens a curiosity-driven conversation.

> "If you're open to a quick call to chat through this, what does your schedule look like?"
> Context: Transitioning from conversation to booking. Use after 4-5 exchanges. Propose specific times next.

## Raw Notes

Alicia runs an SMMA and is documenting her outreach journey. 170 DMs in 4 days. From 100 DMs: 7 seen, 5 positive, 2 declined, rest ghosted. One "I've tried this before" converted into a booked call through genuine curiosity and rapport.

The core lesson is psychological: local businesses (especially contractors) have been pitched constantly and have a high BS detector. The moment they sense a sales pitch, they disengage. A genuine question about their situation bypasses that filter.

Quality score 8: actionable, direct, tested in the field (not theory), and the "no-flip" conversation framework is immediately usable.

## How to Apply

CortexaLabs DM opener for contractors:

"Hi [name], quick question — is your website actually bringing you jobs right now, or is it mostly just sitting there?"

This works because:
- Single question, answerable in a few words
- Doesn't mention our service at all
- Hits the exact pain (website that doesn't generate work)
- Matches contractor directness

If they say "yeah it's fine": "Nice — what's working for you?" (keep the conversation)
If they say "not really": "What does most of your work come from currently?" (discovery → pitch Loom)
If they say "I've tried website companies before": "What didn't work about it?" (curiosity → rebuild trust)
