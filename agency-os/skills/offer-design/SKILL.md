## Value Ladder

```
Entry point: Free preview site + 90-second Loom walkthrough, built BEFORE any pitch or ask for money.
             The prospect sees the finished product first — the video does the selling, not the pitch.
Core offer:  Starter / Pro / Authority tier (one-time fee, see context/offer.md)
Upsell:      Hosting ($97/mo, offered at delivery) → Local SEO ($297/mo) → Google Ads ($497/mo)
```

**Upsell timing insight (from source material):** don't pitch the SEO/ads upsell at close. Wait until the site has been live ~30 days so the client has actually seen it working (or not working) before framing the next step as "now that it's live, here's how to make sure people find it." Pitching immediately at close reads as upselling; pitching after 30 days reads as a natural next step. This sequencing isn't currently specified in `context/outreach-scripts.md` — worth adding to the post-delivery follow-up cadence.

**Stacking logic (validated, not new):** grow revenue by upselling existing clients before chasing new ones. A single client on hosting-only can be walked up through Local SEO and Ads over time — treat every delivered site as an open upsell path, not a closed transaction.

## Positioning vs. Competitors

Already defined in `context/offer.md` under "The competitor close" — pull up the prospect's top local competitor's site during the call and contrast specific gaps. That script is the authoritative version; nothing in the new source material changes it.

**Framing reminder:** sell the outcome, not the deliverable. Prospects don't care about the tech stack or the build itself — they care about more calls/customers. If a prospect pushes back on anything build-related (platform, timeline, tooling), redirect to the outcome rather than defending the build. Don't skip prospects who already have a website if it's weak — pitch a rebuild the same as a no-website prospect.

## ROI Framing

CortexaLabs already has "the math close" in `context/offer.md`. The source material adds a sharper version of the same idea, framed as a question instead of a statement — pulls the number out of the prospect instead of asserting it:

```
ROI Reframe Script (use when they push back on price, or proactively before price is stated):
"Can I ask what one new customer is worth to your business?"
[Let them answer with their own number]
"So if [their fee, $600-$1,100] gets you even one extra job like that, does the math still feel off?"
```

**Per-niche job value reference (cited in source material — verify current against `context/icp.md` before using on a call):**
- Roofing: $8,000–$30,000 per job
- HVAC: $5,000–$20,000 per install
- Concrete: $3,000–$100,000 depending on scope

Pool, landscaping, remodeling, painters figures were not given specific ranges in the source material — still needs research before using this reframe on those niches with confidence.

## Pricing Psychology

**Show before you sell.** The core pricing-psychology device across all three source videos is the same: build the preview site and record the Loom *before* any pitch or price is mentioned. By the time price comes up, the prospect has already seen the finished product and mentally owns it — the conversation becomes "how do we make this live" rather than "should I buy a website." This is the same underlying principle as the DM Sorcery "video does the closing" rule already in `skills/dm-copy/SKILL.md` — treat these as reinforcing the same tactic, not two separate systems.

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Preview site + Loom built and shown before any price is mentioned
- [ ] Offer presented as outcome/result, not features
- [ ] ROI reframe used (their own job value, not an assumed one) before or in response to price pushback
- [ ] Guarantee mentioned to reduce risk (Authority tier only, per `context/offer.md`)
- [ ] Next step is specific (not "I'll send you info")
- [ ] Upsell not pitched at close — held until ~30 days post-launch
