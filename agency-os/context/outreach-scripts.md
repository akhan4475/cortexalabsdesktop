## Section 2 — Reply Handling Scripts

### They Said "Not Really / Mostly Referrals" (Warm Path)
```
Yeah, that's super common for [niche] contractors — most sites are just sitting there not doing anything useful.

What does most of your work come from right now — Google, word of mouth, Nextdoor?
```

[After they answer:]
```
Got it. Contractors in [city] who have a site that leads with real project photos and Google reviews front-and-center tend to be the ones getting called first. I've been helping [niche] businesses with exactly that.

Want me to shoot you a quick 2-min video showing what that could look like for [business name]?
```

### They Said "Actually Yeah / We Do Get Work From It"
```
Nice — what's working for you? Most people I talk to say it's been hit or miss.
```

[Keep them talking. If they mention something weak, pivot:]
```
Interesting — have you ever thought about adding [Google reviews widget / before-after photos / click-to-call] to see if that changes anything?
```

### They Said "I've Tried This Before / Not Interested"
```
That's interesting — what didn't work about it?
```

[Listen. Then:]
```
Yeah, that makes sense. Honestly most websites built for contractors are set up to look good, not to actually generate calls. Different problem to solve. What are you using to get new jobs these days?
```

[After 4-5 exchanges:]
```
If you're open to it — I could show you on a quick 15-min call what the difference looks like for [niche] contractors specifically. When are you free this week?
```

### They Asked "How Much Does It Cost?"
```
Depends on what you need — we've worked with [niche] contractors anywhere from $600 to about $1,100 depending on the scope. Easier to give you a real number on a quick call. Worth 15 minutes?
```

### They Said "Not Right Now / Bad Timing"
```
Totally get it. I'll check back in a few weeks — hope the season's going well.
```

[Set a reminder in CRM for 3 weeks and actually follow up.]

## Section 4 — Loom Video Script

**Total length: 2:00–2:30 max. Face cam on. Screen share their site.**

---

**[0:00 – 0:15] Open — Be Real**

*Do NOT script this word for word. Say it naturally.*

> "Hey [first name], Ayaan here — I know it's kind of random to get a video in your DMs, but I actually put this together specifically for [business name] and I think there's something genuinely useful here. Give me 2 minutes."

---

**[0:15 – 1:00] Pull Up Their Site — Point Out 2-3 Specific Issues**

*Navigate to their actual website or Google listing. Talk through what you see.*

Choose 2-3 that apply:

> **No clear CTA:**
> "So right here on the homepage — there's no button to call or request a quote. A homeowner lands here looking for a [pool/roofer/landscaper], and there's nothing telling them what to do next."

> **No reviews shown:**
> "You've got [X] Google reviews — that's solid — but none of them are showing on the site itself. That's the first thing a customer wants to see before they call."

> **Not mobile:**
> "Most people are going to find you on their phone. Here's what this looks like on mobile... [switch to mobile view] — you can see it's kind of a mess. That's costing you calls."

> **No project photos:**
> "There's no before-and-after or project gallery on here. [Niche] customers want to see your actual work. That's what builds trust before they even pick up the phone."

> **No site at all:**
> "I searched [business name] in [city] — no website comes up. Your competitors who have a site are getting found first. That's a solvable problem."

---

**[1:00 – 1:45] Paint What Good Looks Like**

*Don't pitch. Show or describe the contrast.*

> "Contractors in [city] who have a site that loads fast, shows their actual work, and has their Google reviews front-and-center — those are the ones getting called first when someone searches '[niche] near me.' It's not complicated, it's just done right."

*Optional: pull up a well-built contractor site in the same niche for contrast.*

---

**[1:45 – 2:15] Social Proof (Only If You Have It)**

*One sentence. Skip if you have no client results yet.*

> "I built a site for [similar niche] contractor in [city] earlier this year — they went from zero website calls to getting 3-5 inbound leads a month within the first 60 days."

*If no clients yet — skip and go straight to CTA.*

---

**[2:15 – 2:30] CTA — Invite a Reply**

> "Happy to show you exactly what I'd build for [business name] specifically — just reply to my message and we can find 20 minutes. No pressure."

**Do NOT say "book a call" or show a Cal.com link in the video.**

## Section 5 — Follow-Up Sequences (No Reply)

### Day 3 Follow-Up (after initial DM, no reply)

Pick whichever fits the prospect:

**Option A — Pain Shift Question:**
```
Hey [name] — random one. Do most of your [pool/roofing/landscaping] customers find you through referrals, or do you get much from online?
```

**Option B — Observation:**
```
[Name] — noticed [business name] doesn't have Google reviews showing on the site. Most contractors miss that — it's usually the first thing a new customer looks for. Just thought it was worth flagging.
```

**Option C — Loom Tease:**
```
[Name] — put together a quick 90-sec video on [business name]'s site specifically. Want me to send it?
```

### Day 7 Follow-Up (still no reply)

**Option A — Last Shot:**
```
Hey [name], last message from me — made a quick video showing what I'd change about [business name]'s site to get it bringing in jobs. Worth 90 seconds if you're curious. Want me to send it?
```

**Option B — Walk Away:**
```
Hey [name] — I'll leave you alone after this. I do think [business name] is leaving jobs on the table with the current site, but totally understand if now isn't the right time. Around if anything changes.
```

## Section 7 — Call Scheduling Scripts

### After They Agree to a Call:
```
Great — does [Thursday at 3pm] or [Friday at 11am] work better for you? I'll send over a Cal.com link.
```

*Only send the Cal.com link after they've picked a time.*

### Reconfirmation (Evening Before):
```
Hey [name] — confirming we're still on for [time] tomorrow. Looking forward to it.
```

### Morning Of (If No Reconfirmation):
```
Hey [name] — we're on for [time] today. Talk soon.
```

### No-Show Recovery:
```
Hey [name] — looks like we missed each other. No worries — want to reschedule? I can do [time] or [time] this week.
```
