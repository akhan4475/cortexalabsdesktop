# CortexaLabs AI — Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

## Universal Qualification Rules (All Niches)

A lead must pass ALL of these to enter the pipeline:

- Local business (not a franchise, not a national brand)
- Has a Google Maps / Google Business listing
- Minimum 3 reviews in the last 6 months (total review count 3+, recency matters)
- Google rating 3.8 or above (below this, their service has bigger problems than their site)
- Based in the US
- In one of the 6 approved niches
- Has a real business address or service area listed
- Not already a current client

### Auto-Disqualify (Skip Immediately)
- Franchise or chain location (e.g., "Mr. Rooter," "1-800-Plumber," "HomeTeam Pest Defense")
- Review count of 0–2 (not established enough — they will ghost or can't afford it)
- Rating below 3.8 (service quality issue, not a website issue)
- Business appears inactive (no reviews in 12+ months, no recent Google posts)
- Out of niche (plumbers, electricians, HVAC — not our focus)

## Pool Contractors

### Business Profile
- Revenue range: $250K–$3M/year
- Employees: 3–20
- Services: pool installation, pool renovation, pool cleaning, pool repair
- Job values: $5,000 (repair/cleaning) to $60,000+ (new build)
- Seasonality: Active April–October in northern states, year-round in FL, AZ, TX, CA

### Owner Profile
- Age: 35–58
- Tech-savviness: Low to moderate. Checks email. Has a phone. Does not know what a CTA is.
- How they get clients now: referrals, word of mouth, maybe a HomeAdvisor or Angi listing
- Mindset: skeptical of marketing, but open if you speak to ROI in plain English

### Pain Points
1. Referrals are slowing down and they can't predict next month's pipeline
2. Their site is outdated or embarrassing — they know it but haven't prioritized fixing it
3. Competitors in their area have better-looking sites and are showing up higher on Google
4. They're losing calls to competitors who look more professional online
5. They have great reviews but no way to surface them to new prospects

### What Makes Them Qualified
- 5+ Google reviews with 4.2+ rating
- Has a serviceable but outdated website (good target — they know they have a problem)
- OR: no website at all (good target — the gap is obvious)
- Service area is a real metro (not a rural area with 500 people)
- Appears to be actively booking (reviews are recent, business photos are updated)

### What Resonates in Outreach
- Showing them specifically what their site looks like vs. a competitor
- Mentioning their specific city and pool season
- The stat: "74% of homeowners say they judge a contractor's professionalism by their website"
- Fast turnaround — pool contractors think web projects take forever

## Roofing Contractors

### Business Profile
- Revenue range: $500K–$5M/year
- Employees: 5–30
- Services: roof replacement, roof repair, storm damage restoration, gutters
- Job values: $7,000 (repair) to $25,000+ (full replacement)
- Seasonality: Spring and fall peaks, driven by storm season in many markets

### Owner Profile
- Age: 32–55
- Tech-savviness: Low. Runs the business from a truck. Uses Facebook more than email.
- How they get clients now: door-knocking after storms, insurance adjuster referrals, word of mouth
- Mindset: very ROI-focused, skeptical, moves fast when they see value

### Pain Points
1. Storm season creates a burst of demand they can't capture fast enough
2. Insurance-job leads require professional credibility — a bad site kills trust
3. Competition from large national roofing companies who have big ad budgets
4. Homeowners search online after a storm — if you're not there, you don't get the call
5. No way to capture leads after-hours (no booking widget, no contact form that works)

### What Makes Them Qualified
- Has a truck fleet photo on their Google listing (real operation)
- Reviews mention specific storm events or locations
- Website is absent or has no trust signals (license #, insurance, BBB)
- Business has been operating 2+ years (established, not a fly-by-night)

### What Resonates in Outreach
- Mention storm season and the urgency of capturing leads now
- Show how many people Google "roof repair [city]" every month
- Lead with insurance credibility (homeowners need to trust before filing a claim)
- Speed: "Your competitor's site was done in 10 days"

## Landscaping Contractors

### Business Profile
- Revenue range: $150K–$2M/year
- Employees: 2–15
- Services: lawn care, landscape design, hardscaping, irrigation, seasonal cleanup
- Job values: $500 (weekly maintenance) to $30,000+ (full landscape design/hardscape)
- Seasonality: Spring through fall, some year-round in warmer climates

### Owner Profile
- Age: 28–50
- Tech-savviness: Moderate. Uses Instagram to post job photos. Checks Facebook regularly.
- How they get clients now: yard signs, Instagram, referrals from HOAs
- Mindset: creative, proud of their work, wants clients who appreciate quality

### Pain Points
1. They have beautiful work to show but no professional platform to showcase it
2. Instagram doesn't convert — followers don't become clients reliably
3. HOA and commercial contracts require a professional website to be taken seriously
4. Seasonal lulls make revenue unpredictable
5. Standing out from cheap lawn mowing services is hard online

### What Makes Them Qualified
- Has a portfolio of actual landscaping/hardscaping work (photos on Google or Instagram)
- Mentions design work in their Google Business description (not just "lawn mowing")
- Reviews mention specific project types ("new patio," "full yard redesign," "irrigation install")
- Based in a suburban market with homeowner density

### What Resonates in Outreach
- Show their portfolio photos could be displayed much better
- Mention HOA and commercial contracts as opportunities unlocked by a professional site
- "Your Instagram can't get you a $20K hardscape job, but a website can"

## Remodeling Contractors

### Business Profile
- Revenue range: $300K–$4M/year
- Employees: 3–20
- Services: kitchen remodel, bathroom remodel, additions, basement finishing, whole-home reno
- Job values: $8,000 (bathroom) to $150,000+ (whole-home addition)
- Seasonality: Relatively consistent year-round, slight peak in spring

### Owner Profile
- Age: 35–60
- Tech-savviness: Low to moderate. Has heard of Houzz. May have a Houzz profile.
- How they get clients now: Houzz, referrals, word of mouth, Angi
- Mindset: values craftsmanship, relationship-oriented, distrusts pushy salespeople

### Pain Points
1. Project photos are scattered across Houzz, Facebook, and Google with no central home
2. High-value clients ($50K+ jobs) vet contractors thoroughly before calling — bad sites kill them
3. Houzz takes a large cut and owns the client relationship
4. No way to capture leads who find them on Google after hours
5. Generic contractor site templates make them look like every other remodeler

### What Makes Them Qualified
- Before/after project photos visible on Google or Houzz
- 4.5+ star rating (remodeling clients are vocal)
- Project types mentioned include kitchen, bath, or additions (higher value work)
- Business in operation 3+ years

### What Resonates in Outreach
- Show them a before/after photo of a remodel they did — then show how their website doesn't show it
- "A homeowner planning a $40K kitchen remodel will spend 2+ hours researching before calling. Your current site won't close them."
- Independence from Houzz — own your leads

## General Contractors (Construction)

### Business Profile
- Revenue range: $500K–$10M/year
- Employees: 5–50
- Services: commercial builds, residential construction, project management, specialty trades coordination
- Job values: $20,000 to $1M+ per project
- Seasonality: Weather-dependent but relatively stable

### Owner Profile
- Age: 38–60
- Tech-savviness: Low. Has an office manager who handles "the computer stuff."
- How they get clients now: subcontractor referrals, developer relationships, cold bidding, repeat clients
- Mindset: conservative, relationship-focused, values reputation above everything

### Pain Points
1. RFPs and bids increasingly require a credible web presence
2. Potential commercial clients vet contractors via website before ever making contact
3. Old or broken sites undermine credibility in high-stakes conversations
4. No way to showcase project portfolio to prospective clients without sending PDFs
5. Competitors with better digital presence are winning bids they should be getting

### What Makes Them Qualified
- Has completed commercial or significant residential projects (visible on Google or project photos)
- Business has been operating 5+ years
- License number is verifiable
- Reviews mention specific commercial or large residential projects

### What Resonates in Outreach
- "When a developer Googles your company name before calling you, what do they see?"
- Credibility framing over "more leads" framing — these clients care about perception, not volume
- Project portfolio: we can make their best projects look as impressive as they actually are

## Painters (Interior/Exterior)

### Business Profile
- Revenue range: $100K–$1.5M/year
- Employees: 2–12
- Services: interior painting, exterior painting, cabinet painting, commercial painting
- Job values: $1,500 (interior room) to $15,000 (full exterior + interior)
- Seasonality: Spring through fall peak for exterior, year-round for interior

### Owner Profile
- Age: 28–50
- Tech-savviness: Low to moderate. Active on Facebook. Uses Google Maps to find clients.
- How they get clients now: Nextdoor, Facebook groups, referrals, yard signs
- Mindset: price-sensitive about services they buy, but understand repeat business value

### Pain Points
1. Painting is a highly commoditized service — looking professional online is a differentiator
2. Nextdoor and Facebook get them small jobs, not large whole-house contracts
3. Cabinet painting is a high-margin niche but requires credibility to win (before/after photos, trust)
4. They get ghosted when they send a quote via text with no professional presence to back it up
5. Competitors who look more polished online win the same jobs at higher prices

### What Makes Them Qualified
- Has before/after photos on Google or Facebook showing quality work
- Reviews are recent and mention specific project types
- Business has been operating 2+ years
- Mentions specialty services (cabinet painting, epoxy, commercial) — higher job values

### What Resonates in Outreach
- "Cabinet painters with a professional site are charging 2x what you are for the same work"
- Before/after photo showcase — we make their work look like a portfolio
- "Nextdoor gets you $500 jobs. A website gets you $5,000 jobs."
